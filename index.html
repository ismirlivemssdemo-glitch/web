CrowdioSet and PaRIRset: Two datasets towards Live Music Source Separation

Author1, Author2, Author3
Affiliation1, Affiliation2, Affiliation3

Abstract

Most Music Source Separation (MSS) models do not generalize well to live music recordings because they are trained on studio recordings alone, disregarding the venue acoustics, the speaker system's response and audience noise. We propose to bridge this gap by providing and training a model on two novel datasets.

First, we present PaRIRset, a stereo impulse response dataset captured across 60 professional concert venues using a microphone array. Our results show that adding PaRIRset RIRs increases the performance of MSS models compared to using real general purpose RIRs from Speech Enhancement tasks alone.

Second, we introduce CrowdioSet: a synthetic noise dataset comprising 6000 real ambience tracks from FreeSound as well as synthetic sing-alongs for the vocals in MUSDB18 and MOISESDB datasets, generated from zero-shot singing voice conversions. Training on CrowdioSet allows to separate the audience from live music recordings. We make our baseline model publicly available, as well as PaRIRset and CrowdiosSet.
